1890

Miss Emily’s father, Mr. Grierson, passes away. For three days, she denies his death.

At 72 hours, Mr. Grierson’s body would have bloated significantly. The bloating would have attracted flies to lay larvae. A strong smell would be beginning to emanate as bacteria feed on the matter and release methane-rich gases.

Stromberg, J. (2014, October 28). The science of human decay. Retrieved from https://www.vox.com/2014/10/28/7078151/body-farm-texas-freeman-ranch-decay

1890

The house is the only thing left to Miss Emily. The townspeople feel sorry for her. Emily is roughly 30 years old.

Miss Emily’s home is said to have a big, squarish frame decorated with cupolas, spires, and scrolled balconies in the style of the 1870s. While many of Faulkner’s building descriptions are based on real locations in Mississippi, it is unknown if there is a direct inspiration for the Grierson home. The features are consistent with a Victorian-style architecture, yet the squarish frame is more consistent with the Greek Revival style of the 1860s.

Sanders, T. (n.d.). Architecture in Mississippi: From Prehistoric to 1900. Retrieved from http://www.mshistorynow.mdah.ms.gov/articles/327/architecture-in-mississippi-from-prehistoric-to-1900

1891

Miss Emily meets Homer Barron, a northerner. They are seen riding together in a yellow-wheeled buggy.

Buggy likely refers to a “horse and buggy” as automobiles existed, but were novel at the time.

1892

Miss Emily purchases poison, arsenic. Homer Barron leaves while Miss Emily’s cousins visit.

Arsenic and other poisons had begun to be regulated in the later half of the 19th century. 33 states had adopted pharmacy laws modeled after Great Britain’s 1868 Pharmacy Act by 1890. This law required Pharmacists to be licensed and to record transactions in which poisons, such as arsenic, were purchased. Arsenic gained a great deal of attention following a series of poisoning murders in 1840s England.

1892

Miss Emily purchases a silver toilet set for Homer and an outfit of men’s clothing. The townspeople believe that they have married.

Also known as a “toilet service”, a toilet set consists of items for shaving, bathing, and cosmetic upkeep. During the 19th century, a toilet set plated in gold or silver was a status symbol reserved only for the very wealthy. Affordable, portable, non-plated varieties gained popularity among the middle class in the late 19th century. A toilet service was often a gift to a groom upon marriage.

1892

The cousins leave and Homer Barron returns.

It is probable that the cousins still held distaste for northerners from the post-Civil War reconstruction period twenty years prior (1865 – 1877). Many southerners during that period saw the influx of northerners as trying to take advantage of their post-war disheveled economy. They labeled these northern opportunists as “carpetbaggers” as they would come to the south with all of their possessions in a carpet bag. A southerner, like Miss Emily, who welcomed the northerners and reconstruction would be called a “scalawag”, a term that held the connotation of being a traitor to the south. This may explain why Miss Emily’s relationship with Homer has scandalous connotations with the townsfolk.

(1) Nix, E. (2015, March 03). What's the difference between a carpetbagger and a scalawag? Retrieved November 15, 2020, from https://www.history.com/news/whats-the-difference-between-a-carpetbagger-and-a-scalawag

1892

A smell develops around Miss Emily’s home. Four of the townsmen break into Miss Emily’s cellar and spread lime in attempts to neutralize the smell.

After the death of Miss Emily’s father, it’s odd that no one would recognize the smell or the odd circumstances prior to his funeral.

1892

Two weeks after spreading lime, the smell goes away.

Homer would have had to be dead for 50-365 days to enter the dry decomposition period for the smell to disperse naturally. Due to the humid climate of the south, it could be surmised that Homer’s body would take longer to dry. Prior to the dry period, a decomposing body undergoes putrefaction during which soft tissue is liquified and drained from the body. Therefore, it can be theorized that Miss Emily took action to rid the corpse and house of the smell, or that Homer was dead longer than previously thought.

1894

The Mayor of Jefferson, Colonel Sartoris, informs Miss Emily that she will pay no taxes in Jefferson.

Jefferson, Mississippi is a fictional town based on Oxford, Mississippi. It is located in Yoknapatawpha County, a fictional county based on Lafayette County, Mississippi. William Faulkner authored 14 novels set in this location from 1929 to 1962. Faulkner based many fictional landmarks on real locations in Oxford and lived there until his death in 1962.

1901

Miss Emily gives china painting lessons in the lower floor of her house.

China painting was a popular activity for women in the post-civil war area. Blank china plates were imported from France. A popular technique is to paint the porcelain by hand and fire it in a kiln. Repeating the painting and kiln-firing allows layered applications to become more vibrant.
Presumably, Homer Barron's corpse resides upstairs during these lessons.

(1) Gilbert, A. (2017, June 26). Hand-painted china was a popular hobby. Retrieved from http://www.collectorsjournal.com/columns/hand-painted-china-was-a-popular-hobby/article_45d3cfea-5a75-11e7-a2f4-9fd190694768.html

1908

Miss Emily ceases to give china-painting lessons.

It's not clear why Miss Emily stopped giving china-painting lessons. Amateur china painting existed as a popular hobby in America until the 1920s.

1910

Colonel Sartoris passes away.

Colonel Sartoris is first introduced in William Faulkner’s “Sartoris” (1929). It is the first of Faulkner’s published writing to take place in Yoknapatawpha County. Set after World War 1, it follows the decline of southern aristocracy and the Sartoris family. Colonel Sartoris’ character is based on William Faulkner’s great-grandfather who was a colonel in the confederate army.

(1) Joseph Leo Blotner (1974): Faulkner: a biography. New York, Random House.

January 1, 1919

A new generation of Aldermen and Mayor mail Miss Emily a tax notice.

The real mayor of Oxford, Mississippi at the time was likely Edward D. Beanland. Mayor Beanland only served for about 9 months from January 1919 to September 1919. He built the Beanland-Young house in 1905, which is notable for it’s deviation from the more traditional Victorian and neoclassical style of southern homes prior. The Beanland-Young house follows the “Prairie School” style of architecture, most famously represented in designs by architect Frank Lloyd Wright.

February, 1919

The Mayor writes to Miss Emily personally. She responds in handwritten calligraphy informing him that she does not leave the house.

The present-day City Hall of Oxford Mississippi, (the inspiration for Faulkner’s Jefferson), was the town Post Office and federal building during the early 20th century. It is from this building that a letter to Miss Emily could have been written and delivered from. It is also the building in which Miss Emily could have attended the Board of Aldermen meeting.

A special meeting of the Board of Aldermen is convened. Miss Emily is escorted from her home to attend the meeting.

"Board of Aldermen" is a term synonymous with "City Council". It comes from the Old English title of "ealdorman", meaning “elder man”.
